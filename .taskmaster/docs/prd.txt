# Canonical Product Requirements: Owner-Operated Editorial Magazine

**Status:** Canonical product truth

**Effective:** 2026-07-09

**Program:** Task 33 — Establish SOTA 2026 Magazine Foundation
**Supersedes:** `.taskmaster/docs/archive/2025-legacy-prd.txt`

## 1. Product Definition

Build a premium editorial magazine and blog that a nontechnical owner can operate without developer assistance. The product combines a polished reader experience with an app-owned editorial workspace. Normal publishing must never require Git, Markdown files, terminals, deployment tools, database tools, hosting consoles, or a traditional external CMS.

The application owns its content model, editorial workflow, revisions, media metadata, publishing state, and portable exports. Vendors may provide infrastructure behind explicit adapters, but no vendor may become the only usable copy of the owner's content or media.

## 2. Product Outcomes

The foundation succeeds when:

1. The owner can create, recover, preview, schedule, publish, update, unpublish, and export an article from a browser.
2. Readers receive an art-directed, fast, accessible magazine on mobile and desktop.
3. Editorial content, media originals, metadata, and audit history remain recoverable and portable.
4. The system is simple enough for one owner while preserving a credible path to multiple authors and editors.
5. Development and deployment are measurable, reversible, documented, and protected by automated verification.

## 3. Users

### 3.1 Owner and Editor

The initial owner is not technically proficient. The owner needs clear language, safe defaults, visible publishing state, automatic recovery, and guided corrective actions. Technical implementation details must not leak into the normal workflow.

### 3.2 Reader

Readers arrive through search, social links, newsletters, feeds, direct navigation, and internal discovery. They expect immediate readability, trusted metadata, responsive media, intuitive navigation, and accessible interaction.

### 3.3 Future Contributor

The architecture must support later introduction of authors, editors, and reviewers without redesigning content ownership or audit history. Multi-user workflow is not required in the first product increment.

### 3.4 Maintainer

Maintainers need typed contracts, reproducible environments, meaningful tests, clear migrations, actionable observability, portable deployment, and documented rollback and restore procedures.

## 4. Product Principles

- **Owner-first:** optimize ordinary publishing for a nontechnical person.
- **Content-owned:** retain canonical content, media, metadata, and exports under owner control.
- **Editorial, not generic:** support magazine-quality storytelling and art direction rather than a generic page builder.
- **Safe by default:** autosave, recovery, revision history, previews, validation, and explicit destructive actions.
- **Progressively portable:** isolate provider-specific behavior behind replaceable interfaces and test exports.
- **Accessible and fast:** treat accessibility and Core Web Vitals as release criteria, not cleanup work.
- **Incremental and reversible:** deliver architecture and modernization in independently reviewable batches.

## 5. Owner Experience Requirements

### 5.1 Authentication and Session Safety

- Provide a secure owner sign-in experience with phishing-resistant authentication available.
- Support session expiry, explicit sign-out, recovery, and clear failure states.
- Protect all editorial, media, preview, export, and administrative operations server-side.
- Record security-relevant administrative actions in an audit trail.

### 5.2 Editorial Dashboard

- Show drafts, scheduled, published, unpublished, and recently edited content.
- Provide search and filters by title, author, section, category, tag, and status.
- Surface incomplete metadata, failed publishing jobs, stale drafts, and recoverable autosaves.
- Use plain editorial language rather than database or deployment terminology.

### 5.3 Article Lifecycle

An article must support:

- creation from a blank document or editorial template;
- explicit draft state;
- frequent autosave with visible save status;
- local or server recovery after interruption;
- immutable revision history with comparison and restore;
- private preview that reflects the intended public rendering;
- validation of required editorial and SEO fields;
- immediate publish;
- scheduled publish in an owner-selected time zone;
- update without losing prior revisions;
- unpublish without deletion;
- soft delete and recoverable trash;
- permanent deletion only through a deliberate, confirmed action.

Publishing operations must be idempotent. Repeated jobs or retries must not create duplicate public records, emails, feed entries, or analytics events.

### 5.4 Block Editor

Provide a polished, keyboard-accessible, Notion-like editor with at least:

- paragraphs and headings;
- ordered, unordered, and task lists;
- quotes, pull quotes, dividers, and callouts;
- links and inline emphasis;
- images with captions, alt text, credit, and focal point;
- galleries with ordering and layout choices;
- embeds with safe allow-listing and fallbacks;
- reusable editorial blocks;
- undo and redo across meaningful editing operations;
- paste handling that removes unsafe or unsupported markup;
- clear handling of unknown or migrated blocks.

The canonical document must use a versioned structured representation. Public rendering must be server-safe and must not trust stored HTML.

### 5.5 Media Workflow

- Upload media directly from the editorial workspace.
- Preserve original files separately from generated renditions.
- Record dimensions, MIME type, byte size, checksum, creator credit, rights notes, alt text, caption, and focal point.
- Validate type and size before finalizing an upload.
- Generate responsive delivery formats without replacing the original.
- Show upload, processing, failure, retry, and deletion states clearly.
- Prevent deletion of media still referenced by published content without an explicit resolution path.
- Include originals and metadata in backup and export procedures.

### 5.6 Taxonomy and Editorial Metadata

- Support authors, sections, categories, tags, series, featured status, dek/excerpt, and publication dates.
- Enforce stable, unique slugs while retaining redirects after slug changes.
- Support per-story art direction within controlled templates.
- Provide SEO title, description, canonical override, social title, social description, and social image fields with sensible defaults.

## 6. Reader Experience Requirements

### 6.1 Magazine Presentation

- Deliver premium typography, deliberate spacing, strong hierarchy, and responsive art direction.
- Support homepage, section landing pages, article pages, author pages, category/tag pages, search, and static editorial pages.
- Provide configurable hero, lead-story, grid, list, gallery, related-story, and newsletter modules.
- Preserve reading position and avoid layout shifts as media loads.
- Respect reduced motion, color-scheme preferences, zoom, text reflow, and keyboard interaction.

### 6.2 Discovery and Navigation

- Provide site search across published titles, excerpts, article text, authors, sections, categories, and tags.
- Provide related stories using transparent editorial and taxonomy signals.
- Support breadcrumbs where useful, clear active navigation, and consistent canonical URLs.
- Provide RSS feeds and an XML sitemap.

### 6.3 SEO and Social

- Generate unique titles, descriptions, canonical URLs, robots directives, Open Graph metadata, and social cards.
- Emit valid structured data for articles, authors, breadcrumbs, and the publication where applicable.
- Preserve redirects for changed URLs.
- Prevent drafts, private previews, and administrative routes from indexing.
- Make social-card generation deterministic and testable.

### 6.4 Newsletter

- Collect subscribers with explicit consent and accessible validation.
- Store consent state and subscription history in an app-owned model.
- Support unsubscribe and data export/deletion requests.
- Integrate email delivery behind a replaceable provider adapter.
- Do not make publishing success depend on synchronous email delivery.

## 7. Content and Data Ownership

### 7.1 Canonical Storage

- Store editorial entities and workflow state in an app-owned relational database.
- Store structured article documents in a versioned format with schema identifiers.
- Keep immutable revisions and auditable state transitions.
- Store media originals in owner-controlled object storage behind an S3-compatible abstraction.
- Treat rendered HTML, transformed images, search vectors, feeds, and social cards as rebuildable derivatives.

### 7.2 Schema Evolution

- Every content-document change must define forward migration behavior.
- Migrations must be repeatable, observable, and safe to retry.
- Unknown blocks must fail safely and remain recoverable.
- Destructive migrations require a verified backup and rollback plan.

### 7.3 Export and Import

Provide a documented, versioned export containing:

- content entities and structured documents;
- revisions and essential audit metadata;
- authors and taxonomies;
- redirects and publishing metadata;
- subscriber records subject to privacy controls;
- media manifest, original object identifiers, checksums, captions, credits, rights notes, and focal points;
- schema and export format versions.

The product must prove export and re-import through automated or repeatable verification. Markdown may be offered as a convenience format but is not the lossless canonical representation.

## 8. Search, Analytics, and Observability

- Begin with database-backed full-text and fuzzy search unless measured scale proves an external engine necessary.
- Collect privacy-conscious product analytics with clear retention and consent behavior.
- Capture server errors, publishing-job failures, latency, and relevant traces without storing article drafts or secrets in telemetry.
- Record field Core Web Vitals at the 75th percentile once traffic permits.
- Provide health signals for database, media, scheduling, email, backup, and restore workflows.

## 9. Accessibility and Performance

### 9.1 Accessibility

- Target WCAG 2.2 Level AA for public and owner experiences.
- Test keyboard operation, focus order, focus visibility, labels, error messaging, landmarks, headings, contrast, zoom, reflow, reduced motion, and screen-reader semantics.
- Treat editor accessibility, media alternative text, and owner error recovery as release-critical.
- Combine automated checks with documented manual verification.

### 9.2 Performance

At the 75th percentile, target:

- Largest Contentful Paint at or below 2.5 seconds;
- Interaction to Next Paint at or below 200 milliseconds;
- Cumulative Layout Shift at or below 0.1.

Set page-level budgets for JavaScript, fonts, images, and third-party scripts. Public article rendering must work without shipping editor code.

## 10. Security and Privacy

- Validate authorization for every mutation, preview, export, media, and scheduling operation.
- Use secure cookies, CSRF defenses where applicable, rate limits, input validation, output encoding, and restrictive content security policy.
- Sanitize or structurally render rich content and embeds; never render untrusted stored HTML directly.
- Keep secrets outside the repository and redact them from logs and error reports.
- Maintain dependency and runtime security update procedures.
- Document data inventory, retention, consent, access, export, and deletion behavior.
- Test backup restoration and owner account recovery.

## 11. Deployment and Operations

- Support a managed initial deployment that minimizes owner operational burden.
- Preserve a documented Node-compatible deployment path outside the primary host.
- Use explicit adapters for database, object storage, email, analytics, observability, and scheduling.
- Separate production, preview, and local environments.
- Make database migrations a deliberate deployment step with rollback or forward-repair instructions.
- Maintain automated backups plus an independent owner-controlled backup path.
- Define recovery point and recovery time objectives before production launch.

## 12. Foundation Program Scope

Task 33 delivers an implementation-ready foundation, not the complete magazine. The program includes:

1. canonical product and architecture truth;
2. primary-source stack research and provisional ADRs;
3. focused spikes for the highest-risk assumptions;
4. a reconciled and valid Taskmaster roadmap;
5. a recorded pre-upgrade baseline;
6. reversible modernization of runtime, tooling, framework, styling, design-system, and workspace foundations;
7. an approved content, persistence, authentication, media, search, observability, and deployment foundation;
8. explicit next work for owner publishing implementation.

The program does not include completion of all reader pages, the full owner workspace, production content migration, or retirement of Aegis legacy scaffolding.

## 13. Delivery and Quality Gates

Each implementation batch must:

- start from synchronized, clean `main` on a task-scoped branch;
- document scope, alternatives, risks, rollback, and non-goals;
- use frozen dependency installation;
- pass applicable typecheck, lint, unit/integration tests, package builds, and production build;
- add Playwright smoke coverage where runnable;
- include responsive visual and accessibility verification for UI changes;
- assess performance impact;
- preserve a clean working tree after verification;
- use a reviewed pull request and wait for explicit human merge approval.

Existing baseline failures must be recorded separately from migration regressions. Checks must not be weakened merely to make a migration pass.

## 14. Success Measures

The implementation-ready foundation is accepted when:

- this PRD and linked canonical documents supersede legacy product truth;
- risky architecture assumptions have documented pass/fail spike evidence;
- the Taskmaster graph reflects the new program without erasing legacy history;
- the old repository baseline is reproducibly recorded;
- selected stable runtime, framework, language, styling, design-system, and test foundations are merged through reversible batches;
- content, database, auth, media, scheduling, backup, restore, and export contracts are implemented or proven sufficiently to begin owner-workflow delivery;
- Aegis and readable S:W:H:E evidence describe decisions, verification, pull requests, and merges;
- the next owner-publishing task has clear acceptance criteria.
